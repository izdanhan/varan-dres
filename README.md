**True Name:** Varan Dres

**Alias:** The Ash-Steward (A title he reclaimed to mock his "noble" ancestors).

**Motto:** "Mortality is not a weakness if one has the will to dictate the terms of life and death."

**Background:** Varan views the "beast blood" of werewolves as primitive savagery and the "vampiric curse" as a parasitic reliance on others. To him, true power is **sovereignty**. Varan did not come to Skyrim seeking a cure for mortality through blood. He came to exploit a province in chaos. He saw a civil war, a dragon crisis, and a crumbling social order as the perfect "ash" from which a new Dres hegemony could rise. ## üé≠ Deep-Roleplay Principles

* **The Purity of the Self:** You refuse to be "changed" by outside forces. No Hircine, no Molag Bal. You are the master of your own biology. Your power comes from your **intelligence (Illusion)** and your **leverage (Speech)**.
* **The Meritocracy of Fear:** You don't hate other races because they are "lesser" by birth, but because they are "lesser" by choice. If a Nord is useful, he lives. If an Argonian is a threat, he is extinguished. You treat the world as a chessboard where you are the only player.
* **The Merchant of Misery:** Use the **Anniversary Edition‚Äôs Farming and Business** mechanics. You don't just clear dungeons; you acquire assets. Gold is the most potent spell in your arsenal.
* **Aesthetic of the Aristocrat:** Since you aren't a monster, you must look like a King. Wear fine clothes, the **Tribunal Robes (AE)**, or **Ebony Armor** for intimidation. You are a "refined" menace.

Without the health buffs of a Vampire Lord or the physical resistance of a Werewolf, you must rely on **Illusion** to prevent enemies from ever swinging at you, and **Alchemy/Speech** to dominate the world's systems.

## üèõÔ∏è World Interaction Strategy

* **The Power of the Purse:** Because you are investing in the **Speech** tree (Merchant/Bribery), you should never clear a bounty by going to jail. Always bribe the guards. It reinforces the roleplay that the law doesn't apply to a Dres.
* **Follower Management:** As a mortal, you need "assets." Use your high Speech and gold to hire mercenaries (like **Jenassa** in Whiterun, a fellow Dunmer). Treat them as disposable tools. If they die, you simply buy another.
* **The "Mortal" Limitation:** Since you are using **Linux Mint**, if you use the "Survival Mode" (included in AE), keep Varan's needs met perfectly. A mastermind doesn't go hungry or cold; he has the resources to always stay in the finest inns.

## üß≠ The Living Quest Guide: A Campaign of Influence

### **ACT I: THE INFILTRATOR (Levels 1-15)**

* **The College of Winterhold:** Join immediately. Not for the "glory of magic," but for the library. Focus on **Illusion** training with Drevis Neloren.
* **The Dark Brotherhood:** Your primary source of income and early-game power. Use the contracts to practice killing without being seen. You are the "cleaner" of Skyrim.

### **ACT II: THE INDUSTRIALIST (Levels 16-35)**

* **Goldenhills Plantation (AE):** This is mandatory. You are a Dres; you need a plantation. Instead of slaves, you use "hired help" (who you manipulate through debt). This generates the passive income needed to bribe every guard in Skyrim.
* **The Thieves Guild:** Climb the ranks to unlock the **Fences**. Being able to sell stolen goods to every merchant in the land represents your total economic control.
* **The Civil War:** Join the **Imperials**. Not out of loyalty, but because a stabilized Empire is better for trade and long-term influence than Ulfric‚Äôs chaotic, short-sighted rebellion.

### **ACT III: THE SHADOW OVER SKYRIM (Levels 36-50+)**

* **Ghosts of the Tribunal (AE):** Travel to Solstheim. Reclaim the gear of the old Dunmer gods. You don't worship them; you wear their power as a status symbol to show the local Dunmer who their new master is.
* **Main Quest/Dragonborn:** Finally address the Dragons. You aren't saving the world because it's the "right thing to do." You are saving it because **it belongs to you**, and Alduin is damaging your property.
* **High King of Influence:** Use **Master-level Illusion (Mayhem/Harmony)** to control entire cities. Walk through the streets of Solitude; if anyone speaks out of turn, a whispered word (or a Frenzy spell) ensures they are dealt with by their own neighbors.
