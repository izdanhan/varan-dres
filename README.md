## üß≠ The Living Quest Guide: A Campaign of Influence

### **ACT I: THE INFILTRATOR (Levels 1-15)**

* **The College of Winterhold:** Join immediately. Not for the "glory of magic," but for the library. Focus on **Illusion** training with Drevis Neloren.
* **The Dark Brotherhood:** Your primary source of income and early-game power. Use the contracts to practice killing without being seen. You are the "cleaner" of Skyrim.

### **ACT II: THE INDUSTRIALIST (Levels 16-35)**

* **Goldenhills Plantation (AE):** This is mandatory. You are a Dres; you need a plantation. Instead of slaves, you use "hired help" (who you manipulate through debt). This generates the passive income needed to bribe every guard in Skyrim.
* **The Thieves Guild:** Climb the ranks to unlock the **Fences**. Being able to sell stolen goods to every merchant in the land represents your total economic control.
* **The Civil War:** Join the **Imperials**. Not out of loyalty, but because a stabilized Empire is better for trade and long-term influence than Ulfric‚Äôs chaotic, short-sighted rebellion.

### **ACT III: THE SHADOW OVER SKYRIM (Levels 36-50+)**

* **Ghosts of the Tribunal (AE):** Travel to Solstheim. Reclaim the gear of the old Dunmer gods. You don't worship them; you wear their power as a status symbol to show the local Dunmer who their new master is.
* **Main Quest/Dragonborn:** Finally address the Dragons. You aren't saving the world because it's the "right thing to do." You are saving it because **it belongs to you**, and Alduin is damaging your property.
* **High King of Influence:** Use **Master-level Illusion (Mayhem/Harmony)** to control entire cities. Walk through the streets of Solitude; if anyone speaks out of turn, a whispered word (or a Frenzy spell) ensures they are dealt with by their own neighbors.
